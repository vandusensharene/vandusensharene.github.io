// Key Findings

What the data showed

Growth is concentrated where margins are strongest. Biotechnology combined the highest profit margin (64.4%), a large customer base (97 accounts), and the top attractiveness score in the analysis (0.98) — but only $3.9M in current revenue, signaling clear expansion headroom.

Margin leakage was hiding at the transaction level. 1,254 individual transactions carried negative margins, concentrated in Aerospace & Defense (510) and Paper & Packaging (316). Product-level analysis traced the pattern to Chemical A, which sold at a −21.97% margin into Medical Equipment and −4.31% into Biotechnology.

Concentration risk compounds the problem. Paper & Packaging alone drives 48% of total profit, and Medical Equipment shows the highest revenue volatility in the book (26.8% coefficient of variation) — a diversification concern flagged for leadership.

// Recommendations

The executive action plan

Grow — invest in Biotechnology

Highest margin, largest headroom, strongest attractiveness score. Secondary target: Machinery & Industrial Goods (63.5% margin, 98 customers).

Fix — reprice or discontinue Chemical A

Eliminate the 1,254 negative-margin transactions and exit four structurally unprofitable customers, recovering ~$201K in profit and 1.45 points of margin.

Protect — de-risk the profit base

Reduce dependency on Paper & Packaging (48% of profit), manage Medical Equipment volatility, and expand the California footprint ($4.1M base).
